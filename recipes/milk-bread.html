Japanese Milk Bread

Achieve a perfect loaf every time.

An airy white bread that always tastes as if it is right out of the oven! It's easy to pull part so you can share it with others. This recipe will be using a 9-inch loaf pan, but to achieve a tall loaf an 8-inch loaf pan is recommended.

Ingredients for the starter
  • 1/3 cup (45g) of bread flour
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) of whole milk
Ingredients for the dough
  • 2 1/2 cups (325g) of bread flour
  • 1/4 cups (60g) of sugar
  • 2 tsp (7g) of active dry yeast - equivalent to 1 packet
  • 1 tsp (4g) of salt
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) of warm whole milk + extra for brushing on the unbaked loaf
  • 4 tbsp (60g) of unsalted butter at room temperature, cut into pieces + extra for buttering bowls and pan

Steps

  1. Create the starter.
    • In a small heavy pot, whisk flour, milk, and 1/2 cup (120ml) of water together until smooth.
    • Bring to a simmer over medium-low heat and cook, stirring often, until thick but pourable for ~10 minutes.
    • When ready, scrape into a measuring cup and cover the surface with a plastic wrap.
  2. Making the dough.
    • In a mixer bowl with a dough hook, combine: flour, sugar, yeast, and salt.
    • Mix until evenly combined.
  3. Add egg, milk, and 1/2 cup of the starter.
    • Mix on low speed and knead for 5 minutes.
  4. Add soft butter and knead for 10-12 minutes until the dough is smooth, springy, and a bit tacky.
  5. Punch the dough down.
    • Use your hands to scoop out the dough and place it onto a surface.
    • With a bench scraper / large knife, cut the dough in half.
    • Lightly form each half into a ball, then cover again and let is rise for 15 minutes.
  6. Preheat oven to 350 degrees.
    • Butter up a 9x5in loaf pan while oven is heating up.
  7. Gently roll out one dough ball into a thick oval using a rolling pin.
    • The dough should be moist and no longer sticky.
    • Flour the pin rather than flouring the surface.
      • Roll away from yourself, then pull in until the oval is ~12 inches long and ~6 inches wide.
  8. Fold the top 3 inches down then fold the bottom 3 inches up to make a rough square.
    • Starting from the right edge of the square, roll up the dough into a log.
    • Pick it up and smooth the top with your hands.
    • Place the log in the buttered pan, seam side down and crosswise, nestling it near one end of the pan.
    • Repeat with the other dough ball, placing it near the other end of the pan.
  9. Cover and let it rest for 30-40 minutes.
    • Let rest until the risen dough is peeking over the edge of the pan and the logs are meeting in the center.
    • Brush the tops of the bread with milk and bake on the bottom shelf of the oven until it is golden brown and puffed.
    • This will be 35-40 minutes.
  10. Let it cool in the pan for 10 minutes.
    • Then, move to a wire rack and let the bread cool for at least 1 hour.
    • This allows the crust to soften up and keeps the crumb loftly.
      • If the bread is cut too soon, the air bubbles inside will deflate.
